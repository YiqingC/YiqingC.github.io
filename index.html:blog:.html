#1 My First Computer Science Class

I took my first computer science class in the fall semester of 2014. It was 115 Robots, Games, & Prob Solving taught by prof Armstrong and before that I have never taken any cs classes. There were two major reasons why I did this one. First was the timing since that was the start of my sophomore year and I was just looking for some challenging but still interesting discipline to declare as my major. The second reason actually came from my freshman year’s roommate Yihong. She took the exactly same class during the first semester with the same professor and she told me that is something different from all of the other classes we have learned before. So I took a look at her syllabus, I have to say, the title sounds interesting to me at that time and the little program they did in the lab really looks like a game. It did look appealing to me but I haven’t made up my mind until the next fall semester. Well then, I need to say I was so disappointed because prof Armstrong has changed literally everything in that class including how he gonna teach in class and what we gonna learn from that course, and it was boring. Basically we just sat there and watched he explained python and then did the exact same code on our own and some same stuff in the lab. So obviously I didn’t have the chance to try that little game-like program neither did I like this course. At this point, it may sounds like I will probably not continue with computer science, but, actually I did feel the beauty of this unique discipline. It was my first cs class, therefore, it was a brand new experience to me that the computer can do whatever the task for you if you pass some proper commands. That is the part I have never tried and it make me feel like I can make something by learning this language. As a result, I told myself I would like to try a few more comp science courses and make the decision later. And now in 2017, I am doing the computer science senior seminar and I am glad I hold on straight to the end.
